ALUMINIUM

A BIT ABOUT ALUMINIUM

Aluminium is a strong, soft, cheap and lightweight material, that’s why it is used in so many products worldwide. Like copper, it is a good conductor for electricity and heat. It is mainly used in alloys to make it even stronger, which then in turn is used in the likes of soda cans, car parts and even airplanes. Aluminium is also very easy to recycle and it doesn’t rust because when it comes in contact with air, aluminium creates a layer over itself to prevent corrosion.

HOW DO YOU SAY IT?

al-ew-min-ee-em

WHERE IS IT USED?

Here's some things that aluminium is used in.

SODA CANS

Aluminium is the main metal in creating soda cans. It’s used because it is easy to recycle

TIN FOIL

Although it's called tin foil, it is actually made of aluminium, it is used to keep your sandwhiches fresh for lunch time.

CLEANING WATER

Aluminium sulfate is used in water treatment, to help get rid of potential bad germs.

CAR PARTS

Aluminium is used in a lot of car parts, specially in the bumper of cars because it absorbs shocks well.

AIRPLANES

Airplanes are made of aluminium because it’s so light and durable. It also doesn’t corrode like other metals.
